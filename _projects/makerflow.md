Etsy is great at orders. It tells you what sold, who bought it, and when it needs to ship.

What it doesn't tell you is where that order is in your production process.

Which items have started? Which ones are blocked? Which custom orders need attention next? Which are creeping towards the dispatch date?

If you're doing five orders a week, you can keep that in your head. Maybe a sticky note.

At 20 orders a week, you're guessing. At 50, you're drowning.

### The spreadsheet phase

Most sellers end up in Google Sheets. It works for a while.

You copy order details over, add columns for status, maybe highlight rows by urgency. It's familiar. It's free.

Then it breaks.

Someone forgets to update a row. The sheet gets stale. You're spending more time maintaining the spreadsheet than actually making things. And every time you switch between Etsy and the sheet, you lose a few seconds of context.

Those seconds add up across hundreds of orders.

### The "separate app" problem

The next step is usually a project management tool. Trello, Notion, Asana. Something purpose-built for workflows.

These tools are good at what they do. The problem is they're separate from Etsy. Separate login, separate tab, separate mental model.

You're now running two systems. Your orders live in one place, your production tracking lives in another, and you're the glue holding them together.

For a solo maker or small team, that overhead is real.

### What I created instead

I created [MakerFlow](https://makerflow.app), a Chrome extension that adds production workflow tracking directly to the Etsy orders page.

You don't leave Etsy. You don't learn a new app. You open your orders page and your production steps are right there, on top of the interface you already use all day.

Each item gets tracked through your workflow steps. You define the steps. You move items through them. You see at a glance what's in progress, what's blocked, and what's ready to ship.

### Why it works

The key insight is that Etsy sellers don't need another system. They need a layer on top of the system they already have.

MakerFlow doesn't replace Etsy's order management. It adds the production visibility that Etsy never built.

No imports, no syncing, no data entry. Your orders are already there. MakerFlow just lets you track the making part.
